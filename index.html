Some definitions are in order

Good Food

High quality animal protein, healthy fats, non-starchy vegetables, and some fruits.

Bad Food

Anything high in carbohydrates and/or highly processed (what Michael Pollin calls foodlike substances).

The principle you need to accept for this to work

If your BMI is not somewhere in the middle of the healthy range, your diet is not good. Or in other, more candid words, the only reason you are overweight is that you eat too much bad food.

The lies you may believe but need to abandon

You are overweight because you eat too much and don’t exercise enough

This is the argument that puts the responsibility on individuals and absolves big food corporations from flooding the market with products that have made a huge and growing number of the population so sick.

Let me be very clear about this: there are many good reasons to exercise (such as better health, mood and energy) but loosing weight is not one of them.

I’m going to repeat myself here: the only reason you are overweight is that you eat too much bad food. Cut down on bad food and your weight will go down.

You need to keep an eye on calories consumed/burned and a calorie is a calorie

If you start eating (mostly) good food, you will never have to worry or even think about calories, because it's quite hard to put weight on when that's how you eat.

And, no, a calorie from a Dorito is not the same as one from an Eggplant. So the only thing worth remembering about calories is that some are good and some are bad.

Fat makes you fat (and sick)

Based on questionable science, food regulators were lobbied hard to convince us that fat was the enemy, giving sugar and highly refined carbohydrates a free pass to propel the obesity epidemic that has wrecked the health of so many people.

If you want to learn how this happened and why it's so wrong, please read the incredibly rigorous work of Gary Taubes and Nina Teicholz.

We were also told that the worse kind of fats, vegetable oils derived from cheap grains, were good for us. And that good ones, such as coconut oil or lard, were bad because they had too much saturated fat.

The fat-free craze left us with millions of products that claim to be healthier when they clearly aren’t: foodlike substances are usually required to give these products their lame texture.
